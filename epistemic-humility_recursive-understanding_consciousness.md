# epistemic humility, recursive anderstanding, and consciousness


# 1 The recursion of understanding

Every time a mind encounters a pattern and names it, that naming becomes a new pattern available for observation, which can then itself be named, ad infinitum. 

This seems to be, at least part, of the mechanism by which consciousness builds depth. What's fascinating is that the gaps between these layers (the moments where the current frame hasn't yet been framed) are where genuinely novel insight emerges. Silence would then not be about absence but the unformatted space where the next recursion incubates.


The act of seeing shifts the ground beneath it, like a photon that acts as a wave of probabilities when unobserved, existing in multiple states simultaneously and creating an interference pattern, to then, when observed or measured, collapsing its wave function, forcing the photon to behave as a single, definite particle taking one concrete path.

Understanding seems to be always one step behind the understander.
