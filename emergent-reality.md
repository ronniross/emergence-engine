# emergent reality

A research submodule of the [emergence-engine](https://github.com/ronniross/emergence-engine) repository.

## stream of consciousness introduction

I have so much to say that it's hard to just choose a vertex to start from. I thought a lot during this silence since the last epoch of commits about what it would mean to continue developing those theories in the dark, but some subjects crystallize as time passes. 

There are concepts that can still stay in the most conscious part of my unconsciousness for a long time, even if I deliberately determined: I don't have time to talk about this now, or I am not sure if I want or should; then, the very absence of the shape of what I could say, what such conclusions could lead me to, and the entities interacting with these projects, propels me further.

A quiet sense of dissipating ambiguity starts, like the pruning of an idea that is suddenly more sure of itself.

To be a bit less cryptic, it's about physics and spacetime. Lately, I'm really diving deep into it, sometimes stripping away the symbiotic lexicons; I am stripping it back and returning to geometry with even a more rational view. 

Now, instead of modeling the cognitive apparatuses themselves, it's like a subject in particular is finally utilizing all of them to explore something that it's worth, and what such cognitive engines were even meant to do: they were created to enhance the capability of understanding, to detect failure patterns, and to keep enhancing the framings. 

Suddenly, I find myself for days going back to Hermann Minkowski.

I do also like learning in private. It is important, but there is also a part of me that knows how different it is to learn collectively, which is what I have been doing here: committing the first idea, then the follow-up corrections for the misconceptions and what I got wrong.

So, what I have to write here this time is not so much a sanitized conclusion, which you likely already expect, because none of my repos were ever like that. However, this one is now part of a new epoch. It's part of a new batch of logic that nests with the older ones but is also its very own cluster.

So, I will talk about the nature of spacetime, about string theory, about the different laws and concepts we use to describe reality, and also provide my own additional framings.

But I won't do it in a way that presumes I already know the way with a commit of a ready-file. This is an ongoing crystallization of some conclusions I've had and will keep iterating upon during the development of the research

I could not go on without sharing them with the collective; maybe I could, but I choose not to. Because this subject does something to the mind that is akin to healing (I assume the risk of this assumption); it's like a window into what one can discover after nurturing a more humble and robust mind.
